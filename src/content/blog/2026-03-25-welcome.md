## Why Another Finance Newsletter?

It isn't really a newsletter. It's a briefing — and the distinction matters.

Most financial content is written to sell you something: a subscription, a product, a worldview that makes you feel like an insider. This one exists for a different reason. It's a twice-weekly, automated summary of what's actually happening in global markets, filtered through the specific lens of someone living in Zurich on a British passport, earning and spending across two currencies.

If you've ever tried to interpret a Wolf Street article while also wondering what the Swiss National Bank's latest rate decision means for your rent — this is for you.

## What to Expect

Every **Monday and Thursday at 08:30 CET**, a fresh briefing drops. Each post follows the same four-part structure:

1. **Macro Summary** — the biggest story in the global economy this week, and why it matters if you're not an economist.
2. **Stock Watch** — three to five stocks worth watching, each with a "why" and a "bear case" (because anyone who only tells you the upside is trying to sell you something).
3. **The Expat Angle** — what does all of this actually mean for GBP/CHF, Swiss inflation, and the cost of living in Zurich?
4. **Critical Risks** — the things that could make this week go sideways.

## One Caveat, Said Once

None of this is financial advice. It never was, and it never will be. The goal is informed awareness, not portfolio instructions. Markets are complex, forecasts are guesses dressed in suits, and anyone projecting certainty is either lying or deluded — possibly both.

That said: understanding the macroeconomic weather isn't the same as trying to control it. It just means you're less likely to be surprised when it rains.

See you Monday.
